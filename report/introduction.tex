The hacker used the model to look for publicly available information about security vulnerabilities in software used by various organizations, find companies that utilized the software, and then exploited the bug to gain access to their internal infrastructure. The hack was unique in the sense that the hack was made with minimal human involvement, and the hacker was able to use the model to generate the necessary malware to extort the companies for a ransom.

If AI continues to develop at the current pace, it is likely that we will see more cases of "vibe hacking", and maybe even cases where the bad actor itself is an AI model, and not a human. If such is the case, then we will need to study how AI models might interact with each other in a local system, to get a feel for their capabilities and limitations, and to be able to train a model that can detect and counteract malicious AI models.

\subsection{The Problem}
\label{ResearchQuestion}

Keeping it simple, the problem we are trying to solve is two part. First, it is uncertain what commands are available for an AI model to use, should it be running on a local system with free reign to interact with the system however it sees fit. Second, it is not known how an AI model should be trained to use these commands, and what the most effective combination of commands would be to counteract another AI model.

Then the problem becomes that we need to know what the options are for an AI that runs on a local system to interact with the system and with other AI models. 

\subsubsection{Research Question}

The research question is then as follows:

\begin{center}
    \textit{How does AI interact with other AI models in a local system, and what are the implications for detecting and counteracting malicious AI?}
\end{center}

The research question is focused on the training aspect of AI, as it is the core problem needs to be solved in this case. Data is the most important aspect of training an AI model, and in our case, it us not clear what that data would look like or how we would obtain it to begin with, as such this becomes the main focus of our research to uncover. 

For this, we will need to explore three main topics. The first being hacking in general, the second being AI and how it is being trained currently, and the third being the intersection of the two, which is "vibe hacking".

With this outline, we can ask ourselves the following sub-questions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item What are the current methods of training AI models?
    \item What are the potential challenges in training an AI model on data indirectly or directly related to another model?
    \item What problems may arise from training an AI to detect and counteract malicious AI, and how can we mitigate these problems?
\end{itemize}
